# T-Spot Trading Model EA for MetaTrader 5

A complete MQL5 Expert Advisor implementing the T-Spot pattern detection and automated trade management system. The EA monitors HTF (default M15) candles for six sweep-based pattern types, enters via limit orders at the logarithmic midpoint of the sweep candle, manages stop loss using FVG proximity or candle-extreme fallback, and drives the position through a five-state lifecycle (None, Pending, Full, Partial/BE, Trailing) with CISD projection targets. The on-chart dashboard provides real-time status.

**Watch for:** Invalidation check fires on every tick during STATE_FULL and STATE_PENDING using M1 close data, which can prematurely close a position on a single M1 wick close beyond the midline (confirmed). The FVG scan direction is inverted relative to conventional ICT FVG definitions, finding gaps *in the direction of entry* rather than the direction of stop protection (confirmed). State sync on re-initialization cannot recover STATE_PARTIAL or STATE_TRAILING, so an EA restart mid-trade drops advanced management (confirmed).

## High-level view

The detection engine evaluates six distinct T-Spot patterns on the last three closed HTF candles, gated by a swept-both invalidation filter and an optional directional bias. The patterns are structured as a priority chain (first match wins), with "Normal" patterns checking single-candle liquidity sweeps, "Expansive" patterns looking at two-candle momentum sequences, and "Pro-Trend Mid Sweep" patterns detecting intra-range liquidity grabs. The logarithmic midpoint calculation weights the entry toward the dominant wick rather than using a naive arithmetic center.

The FVG stop-loss logic scans M1 bars looking for price gaps near the entry, but the search semantics are counterintuitive: for a bearish trade it searches for bullish FVGs above entry (meaning price vacuums that could pull price into the stop zone), rather than bearish FVGs that would act as resistance protecting the stop. This may be intentional (placing the SL beyond a gap that price must re-fill to hit the stop), but it diverges from the conventional "stop behind the FVG" approach and warrants verification against the Pine Script source.

Position management follows a linear state machine: pending order until fill or HTF close, full position until the 2.0 projection level triggers a 70% partial close and breakeven move, then a trailing phase activates at the 2.5 level. The invalidation mechanism (M1 close beyond midline) applies uniformly across STATE_PENDING and STATE_FULL, creating an aggressive early-exit bias that could whipsaw the EA on volatile instruments.

The CISD projection calculation has an index aliasing issue in how it copies HTF bars and interprets them relative to the detection candles, which could miscalculate the series range on certain bar configurations.

<summary>Issues (7)</summary>

1. **Invalidation whipsaw on M1 close** — `CheckInvalidation()` exits the trade if any single M1 bar closes beyond the midline. For a 15-minute HTF, this means a 1-minute wick can exit a trade. Consider gating invalidation on HTF close or requiring N consecutive M1 closes.
2. **FVG search direction semantics** — For bearish entries, the code finds bullish FVGs above entry for stop placement. Verify this matches the Pine Script intent; conventionally the stop goes behind a same-direction FVG that acts as a barrier.
3. **State sync loses advanced states** — `SyncStateFromMarket()` only recovers STATE_PENDING and STATE_FULL. If the EA restarts while in STATE_PARTIAL or STATE_TRAILING, the position reverts to STATE_FULL, losing breakeven protection and trailing logic.
4. **CISD projection bar indexing** — `CopyRates(_Symbol, HTF_Timeframe, 1, 10, htf_bars)` copies bars starting from index 1 (last closed), so `htf_bars[0]` is the same as `g_htf_rates[1]`. The comment says "start from prev_closed" but `start_idx = 1` actually starts from `g_htf_rates[2]`'s equivalent. The series detection may skip or double-count a candle depending on timing.
5. **Partial close position ticket invalidation** — After `PositionClosePartial`, the remaining position may receive a new ticket on some brokers (netting accounts won't, but hedging might). The code continues using `g_position_ticket` without re-querying.
6. **Limit order placement fallback** — When entry price is on the wrong side of current price, the code adjusts to `ask + 1 point` or `bid - 1 point`. This places the limit 1 point from market, virtually guaranteeing immediate fill at a suboptimal price rather than waiting for a pullback. A market order with the original SL would be more transparent.
7. **No maximum SL distance guard** — If no FVG is found and the fallback uses `g_tspot_high + buffer`, a large HTF candle could produce an enormous SL distance. The lot calculator will shrink position size accordingly, but there's no "skip this trade" threshold.

## Entry price divergence from invalidation reference

When the computed midline sits on the wrong side of the current bid/ask (e.g., a bullish signal but midline is above bid), the code adjusts entry to `bid - 1 point`. This creates a limit order that will fill on the next downtick of any size. The original midline is still stored in `g_midline` and used for invalidation checks, meaning the invalidation reference and the actual entry diverge. A position entered at `bid - 1 point` could be immediately invalidated if the midline is above bid and M1 closes below it. If the intent is "only enter at the midline," skipping the signal would be more consistent than forcing a near-market entry.

## FVG stop-loss construction

The FVG scan operates on the most recent `FVG_Lookback_Bars` M1 candles (default 100). For a bearish entry it searches for bullish FVGs (gap up: `bar[i].low > bar[i+2].high`) above the entry price. The definition with the array set as series means bar[i] is more recent and bar[i+2] is older, so this finds a gap left between two older candles as price moved up. Placing the stop *above* a bullish FVG means the stop sits in open air where price has already demonstrated it can reach. The conventional placement would put the stop above a *bearish* FVG (or order block) that should act as resistance.

If no FVG is found, the fallback uses the T-Spot candle's extreme with no cap on distance from entry. On a large engulfing candle, the SL distance could consume the entire risk budget at minimum lot size, or produce a fractional lot below `SYMBOL_VOLUME_MIN`, which would be rounded up to minimum lot and over-risk the account.

## Invalidation aggressiveness in STATE_FULL

`CheckInvalidation()` reads `CopyRates(_Symbol, PERIOD_M1, 1, 1, m1)` — the last *closed* M1 bar. During STATE_FULL, this fires every tick. A single M1 bar closing 0.1 pip beyond the midline will close the entire position at market, regardless of how the HTF candle is forming. On volatile instruments (gold, indices), M1 bars routinely wick through levels and close beyond them before the HTF resolves in favor.

In STATE_FULL the invalidation check runs *before* the partial-close logic, meaning a position that would have hit the 2.0 projection target on the same tick gets closed at a loss or small profit instead. The invalidation is not called in STATE_PARTIAL or STATE_TRAILING, so the aggressiveness is concentrated in the earliest and most vulnerable phase of the trade.

## CISD projection bar indexing

`CalculateProjections` calls `CopyRates(_Symbol, HTF_Timeframe, 1, 10, htf_bars)`. With `start_pos=1`, `htf_bars[0]` after `ArraySetAsSeries` is the last closed bar (the sweep candle). The code sets `start_idx = 1` and iterates from `htf_bars[1]` (two periods ago), but the comment claims "htf_bars[0] = last_closed in our copy starting from 1" — the copy *does* start from bar index 1, so `htf_bars[0]` is `g_htf_rates[1]` (the sweep candle itself), not prev_closed.

The series detection loop's `i > start_idx` guard means the first iteration at `start_idx` always contributes to the high/low calculation regardless of polarity. If the sweep candle's body is opposite polarity, it still inflates `series_high`/`series_low` before the break fires. This produces projection targets further from entry than intended on configurations where the sweep candle opposes the series direction.

## State sync on restart

`SyncStateFromMarket()` recovers STATE_PENDING and STATE_FULL only. If the position was in STATE_PARTIAL (70% closed, BE stop in place), the EA restarts into STATE_FULL and will attempt another 70% partial close if the 2.0 level is re-crossed — taking 70% of the remaining 30%, leaving ~9% of original size. It also re-enables invalidation logic that should have been disabled post-partial.

A persistence mechanism via `GlobalVariableSet`/`GlobalVariableGet` or file I/O would preserve the state machine phase across restarts.
